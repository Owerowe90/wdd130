Intro To Fly Fishing:

Fly fishing uses a very different rod than a normal fishing pole. Both need bait to try and attract the fish to take a bite out of whatever is on the hook. Normal fishing is comprised of either bait and bobber fishing or using a spinner to attract the fish to eat the hook. Fly fishing is very different and requires skill to imitate insects as they either hatch or live in the river. You can fly fish almost any river because you have flies that are either in the water or outside depending on the time of year. Fly fishing Equipment: A fly rod is used when fly fishing and is a lot longer than a normal fishing pole and comes in different lengths and weights. The weight and length of the rod depend on what type of fishing you are doing. The rod is used to direct and cast the line and fly to specific spots. The fly reel holds the different lines used for fishing and the kind of reel you use depends on the weight of your rod. The reel’s purpose is to attach the fly to the rod so that it can be directed as you cast. The reel holds three different types of fly lines. The first is a backing line and is what attaches all the other lines to the reel with an arbor knot. The second is called the fly line and is normally different bright colors, buoyant thicker. It is the reason why you can cast a light fly far distances with a lot of velocity. The third is called a leader and is what is used for accuracy with casting. The fourth is called tippet and is a thin line that sinks due to the weight of the fly. The fly is then attached to the tippet, and you can use tippet to attach multiple flies together. There are different sizes of tippet and leaders. The smaller the number, the thicker the line. So, if I have a 5x leader with a 1x tippet, that would be horribly unbalanced and the chances of you breaking the leader is likely. The most important part of the rig is the fly. Without the fly, you cannot catch any fish. Knowing what type of fly to use is very important. Waders are also important but not necessary. Just allows for better access to certain spots.
